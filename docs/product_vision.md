# Product Vision: Sendoomi

## 🏷 Origins of the Name
**SEN** (Special Educational Needs) + **doomi** (a phonetic play on "to me").
It’s specifically designed for children who might find the "T" sound (as in "Send to me") difficult to pronounce, particularly those with non-verbal attributes or speech delays.

## 🎯 The Core Problem: Overwhelming "Gift" Experiences
Standard e-commerce is predatory:
- **Noise:** Pop-ups, "Other people also bought," "Limited time only."
- **Complexity:** Complex navigation, filters, search bars, and "Add to Cart" flows.
- **Access:** Most shopping apps require an adult's login and credit card, which creates a barrier to a child's autonomy.

## ✅ The Sendoomi Solution
A **"Walled Garden"** where the parent is the gatekeeper of content, but the child is the master of choice.

- **Curated Input:** Parents find things the child *might* like (toys, activities, trips, fruit) and "Share" them to Sendoomi.
- **Simplified Output:** The child is presented with a distraction-free binary choice (Yes/No, This/That).
- **Communication Bridge:** For children who struggle with verbal communication, Sendoomi acts as a digital PECS (Picture Exchange Communication System) board for real-world desires.

## 👥 Personas

### 👩‍💼 The Parent/Carer (The Gatekeeper)
- **Goal:** Curate a safe list of potential positive experiences or items.
- **Workflow:** Browses the web, uses the "Share" feature on their mobile to add links/photos to Sendoomi, and toggles "Kid View."

### 🧒 The Child (The Player)
- **Goal:** Exercise agency and communicate what they want *right now*.
- **Workflow:** Interacts with a calm, neutral interface. Selects "This One" (Green) or "Not This" (Red).

### 👵 The Extended Family (The Giver) - *V1.5+*
- **Goal:** Access the child's "Choice list" to buy something they *know* the child wants.
- **Workflow:** Receives a shared link or invite to view the curated/chosen list.
